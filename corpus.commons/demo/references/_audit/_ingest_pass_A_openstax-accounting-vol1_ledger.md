## Front-matter framing notes

- The book is **Volume 1** of a two-volume set. Volume 1 covers **financial** accounting (external reporting); Volume 2 covers **managerial** accounting (internal). This volume has 16 chapters; the same Vol 1/Vol 2 architecture is referenced in the Preface (lines 519-522 onward).
- The book is "designed to appeal to both accounting and non-accounting majors" (Preface) — pitched as introductory, with frequent recourse to "Your Turn" exercises and "Concepts In Practice" boxes that connect to real companies.
- The book emphasises *stakeholders* explicitly — Ch 1.4 is titled "Explain Why Accounting Is Important to Business Stakeholders" with subsections on Stockholders, Creditors and Lenders, Governmental and Regulatory Agencies, Customers, Managers and Other Employees. This is not boilerplate framing; the book treats the multi-stakeholder audience of financial reporting as a structural premise.
- The book carries an explicit ethics emphasis: fraud (Ch 8.1), internal controls (Ch 8.2-8.5), Sarbanes-Oxley (Ch 8.7), earnings management (Ch 9.4) all surface managerial-decision and stakeholder-trust dimensions.
