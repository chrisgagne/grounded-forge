## Front-matter framing notes

The Preface frames the book as "brief introductory" and "streamlined," covering "key concepts necessary to establish a strong foundation in the subject," with each chapter offering "learning objectives, explanatory narrative and concepts, references for further reading, and end-of-chapter questions." It explicitly states the book may need supplementing — i.e., it is foundational, not exhaustive.

The author roster is unusually employment-and-business-school-tilted (lead author is a practising employment lawyer; contributors span business, employment, and education programmes), which shapes the book's tone: practical, US-centric, weighted toward employment/contract/regulatory material rather than constitutional/criminal theory. No prefatory thesis statement; the book is descriptive rather than argumentative.
