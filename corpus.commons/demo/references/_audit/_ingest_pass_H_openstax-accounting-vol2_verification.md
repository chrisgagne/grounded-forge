## Methodology refinements specific to accounting-vol2

These observations from this ingestion may help future text-and-table-heavy book ingestions:

1. **OpenStax accounting books use typeset tables, not bitmap tables, for most computations.** This means image extraction yields a much lower substantive-image count than for narrative books like Organizational Behavior. The 4 per cent substantive rate here (versus 65 per cent in OB) is not a defect of this ingestion — it reflects the source's compositional style. The deep ref's Key Statistics table and inline computation paraphrases capture what the table images would have captured anyway.

2. **Pass G.0 for stakeholder-engagement on managerial accounting is non-trivial.** The original ingest agent's clear-yes call is defensible because Ch 13 is dedicated to stakeholder accountability and Ch 12.4 explicitly invokes the stockholder-vs-stakeholder distinction. But the volume's centre of gravity is internal decision-making, and the SE projection necessarily leans on a smaller fraction of source content than the DM projection. Future managerial-accounting ingestions should anticipate this asymmetry and consider whether to honour the spawn-prompt's clear-no hint or follow the source-material evidence as this ingestion did.

3. **The five load-bearing claims framing in the deep ref's thesis works well for textbook synthesis.** Reducing thirteen chapters to five organising claims (decision-relative cost classification; short-term vs long-term frame distinction; financial-measures-alone insufficiency; allocation effects on decisions; sustainability as mainstream stakeholder accountability) gave the rest of the deep ref a coherent spine. Other textbook ingestions might consider a similar 4-7 claim structure.

4. **The volume's "Continuing Application" thread (Gearhead Outfitters, an outdoor-equipment retailer)** weaves through chapters but is referential rather than load-bearing for the deep ref's framework synthesis. It is mentioned in passing but not extracted as a primary case study. Subsequent application-file work could revisit it as a unifying example.

5. **Cross-volume alignment with OpenStax Vol 1 (Financial Accounting):** several Vol 2 chapters reference financial-accounting concepts (GAAP, financial-statement analysis in App A, the financial / managerial distinction in Ch 1.2). Vol 1 is also in the Phase 2 batch; the parent agent's consolidation should ensure SEARCH-INDEX cross-references between Vol 1 and Vol 2 are coherent (e.g., the financial-statement-analysis tools in Vol 2 App A overlap with Vol 1's financial-statement chapters).
