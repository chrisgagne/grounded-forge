# Source: The Approach Perfect Field Guide to Scrum Events (Gagné, 2020)

A 17-page practitioner field guide to the five canonical Scrum events plus Backlog Refinement, written by Chris Gagné of Approach Perfect, Limited and published 3 April 2020. The guide is a ceremony-by-ceremony reference, with each event covered under a uniform set of headings—Goal(s), Timing, Participants, Inputs, Output(s), Sample Agenda, Tips—and an "at a glance" summary table on page 2 covering all six events. The Field Guide pairs naturally with the canonical Scrum Guide already in this corpus: the Scrum Guide *defines* Scrum (rules and accountabilities); the Field Guide *instructs* on running it (facilitation depth, sample agendas, time-allocations, named tips). **Provenance.** Chris Gagné is the founder of Approach Perfect, Limited (Wellington, New Zealand), a coaching and consulting practice in Agile and organisational learning. The Field Guide is his practitioner-facing reference for the five Scrum events plus Backlog Refinement, written in 2020 to provide what the Scrum Guide intentionally leaves out: facilitation depth, sample agendas, time-allocation tips, and field-tested practices. The April 2020 edition is the version cited by `ap.tips/eventsfieldguide`.

**Role in corpus.** Scrum-events facilitation-depth resource and the author-perspective complement to the canonical Scrum Guide 2020 (Schwaber & Sutherland) already in this corpus. Where the Scrum Guide *defines* the framework (what each event is, what it produces, what timebox limits it), the Field Guide *instructs* on running the framework: how long Sprint Planning typically takes per Sprint-week, what a sample Sprint Retrospective agenda looks like, named techniques to use (Fists of Five, Planning Poker, Affinity Estimating, the Retrospective Prime Directive, the Vegas Rule, the Chatham House Rule), and field-tested tips for each ceremony.
