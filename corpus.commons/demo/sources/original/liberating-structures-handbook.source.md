# Source: Engaging Everyone with Liberating Structures Handbook (Group Jazz, 2010)

The 2010 Group Jazz handbook *Engaging Everyone with Liberating Structures*, a 60-page pattern-language catalogue of Liberating Structures (LS) interaction designs. Compiled by Lisa Heft and Anne Pattillo as a reminder/reference for participants who have already attended an LS-based event; describes itself in the introduction as "a helpful reminder, not complete instructions." Predecessor to the more widely cited Lipmanowicz & McCandless 2014 trade book *The Surprising Power of Liberating Structures* (which is all-rights-reserved); this earlier handbook is the openly licensed primary source for the same lineage of methods. **Provenance.** The handbook is a Group Jazz publication compiling materials from the Liberating Structures community. The introduction credits the underlying ideas to Henri Lipmanowicz and Keith McCandless and acknowledges that the handbook itself is a participant-facing reminder document. The "Origins" section traces the term *liberating structures* to William Torbert (*The Power of Balance*, Sage, 1991), with Edward de Bono's *Teaching Thinking* (Penguin, 1991) providing a complementary distinction between restricting and liberating structures. The connection between liberating structures and process design is attributed to facilitators and organisational-development practitioners working with large-group methods over the preceding 20 years; Dick Axelrod's *Terms of Engagement* (2nd ed., Berrett-Koehler, 2010) is named as a contemporary articulation. The pattern-language framing borrows from Christopher Alexander (*A Pattern Language*, Oxford U, 1977) and from Peter and Trudy Johnson-Lenz's work on rhythms, boundaries, and containers as social-design primitives.

**Role in corpus.** Activity-catalogue / pattern-language source for group-engagement work. The handbook describes roughly 32 named LS interaction designs at one-to-two-page length each, plus front-matter framing on ownership-vs-buy-in, space and aesthetics, storyboard planning, and the principle of "Nothing About Me Without Me" / "Engage the Unusual Suspects." Maps onto facilitation and stakeholder-engagement work across many task domains; for the corpus's existing two task axes—`decision-making` and `stakeholder-engagement`—both fire (decision-making for the explicit collective-decision tools 25-to-10 and Wise Crowds and for the underlying ownership-vs-buy-in stance; stakeholder-engagement strongly, since the entire handbook is about how to engage everyone in a conversation or design).
