# Source: A Just Culture Guide (NHS Improvement)

A single-page A3 landscape poster (842×1191 pt) issued by NHS Improvement in March 2018. The publication is structurally one document—a decision-tree poster—not a multi-page report; the one-page page-count is the intended shape of the source and not an indication of partial extraction. The full content (four "please note" caveats, three preamble paragraphs framing purpose and use, five sequential tests with their sub-questions, and seven distinct recommendation end-states) is present on the single page and has been preserved in the converted markdown at `../converted/nhs-just-culture-guide.md`.

The guide is attributed on the poster to "the work of Professor James Reason and the National Patient Safety Agency's Incident Decision Tree". It is the clinical-services rendering of the just-culture decision tree: a one-page operational decision aid used by NHS managers to evaluate whether an individual member of staff involved in a patient safety incident requires individual support or intervention to work safely, versus whether a wider systemic investigation is the right response. The poster's working assumption—stated explicitly in the preamble—is that "action singling out an individual is rarely appropriate" and that most patient-safety issues have "deeper causes" requiring "wider action".

The canonical publisher page at improvement.nhs.uk has been retired (NHS Improvement merged into NHS England in 2022); the PDF retrieved for ingestion is hosted as a mirror at cpcw.org.uk and matches the canonical printed poster. The poster's footer credits `improvement.nhs.uk` as the source domain.
