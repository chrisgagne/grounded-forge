# Source: Org Topologies Primer 2025 (Krivitsky, Larman & Flemm)

The 2025 Edition of the *Strategic Org Design: The Primer*, a 27-page primer introducing the Org Topologies™ framework — a two-axis map (scope of skills mandate × scope of work mandate), sixteen named archetypes (CAPS-2, WHOLE-3, TASKS-1, PART-1 …) organised into four archetype groups (Directing, Doing, Delivering, Driving), and three named topologies (Resource, Delivery, Adaptive) — together with a four-step change method (Map → Assess → Design → Elevate, the MADE method) and the Elevating Katas™ as named-but-deferred change practices. The 2025 Edition is distinguished by its explicit treatment of AI as a workforce element and by an updated map that broadens applicability "from product development to service organizations". The Primer is positioned as a *forerunner* to a forthcoming book ("we'll save that for the book we're writing", p. 1) and is explicit about what it does *not* address (the full Elevating-Kata catalogue; multi-goal conflict handling).

**Provenance.** The 2025 Edition is an update over a previous version, described in the Primer as: "The updated Org Topologies map—represented and analyzed on the next page in detail—aims to enhance clarity, broaden applicability from product development to service organizations, and support modern organizational needs such as the adoption of AI. The terminology has been simplified, the visual design refined, and new archetypes for scalable capabilities have been introduced" (p. 4). **Role in corpus.** Strong-fire on `decision-making`, `stakeholder-engagement`, and `software-business`; narrow but useful applicability on `aar` (org structure as contributory factor in Phase 2 contributory-factor analysis; org-design escalation framing in Phase 4 action design) and `retro` (team-archetype-mismatch as a recurring-topic pattern in Phase 3 insight; 15%-experimentable vs structural-escalation distinction in Phase 4 experiment design). The named-archetype vocabulary (CAPS-2, WHOLE-3, TASKS-1) is a corpus-level naming infrastructure that complements OpenStax Principles of Management's structure catalogue (Mintzberg, six structures) and SSDL's system-archetype catalogue, sitting at the *strategic-design stance* level rather than the *structure-catalogue* or *dynamic-archetype* levels. The Primer's mapping of OT's horizontal axis to Scrum's Definition of Done is the explicit conceptual bridge to the Scrum Guide (also CC BY-SA 4.0 in this corpus).
