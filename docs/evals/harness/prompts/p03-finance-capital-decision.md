# Capital decision with NPV positive but real-options reasons to wait

**Domain:** decision-making
**Strength claim:** The Principles-of-Finance distillation consolidates TVM, NPV, IRR, MIRR, payback, capital structure, WACC, CAPM, and real-options thinking into one distillation shaped for a decision-maker. A raw-textbook method has the chapters but not the diagnostic frame; a naive method is likely to give a generic NPV-rules answer without the qualifications.

We are evaluating a $4M capital project. NPV is positive at our current WACC (8.5%), IRR is 14%, payback is 3.2 years on a 6-year asset life. Two complications: (1) regulatory uncertainty in the target market — there is a real chance of policy change in 18 months that would either kill the market or 2x our returns; (2) we have a competing internal proposal for a $4M software platform that would not show NPV positive for two years but is foundational to other work in the pipeline.

What questions should we be asking before the investment committee meets next week? What is the right framework for thinking about the optionality in (1)? How should the existence of (2) influence our decision? What are we likely to get wrong, and how would we know?
