# Revenue recognition judgement call with stakeholder implications

**Domain:** decision-making
**Strength claim:** Cross-source synthesis question — Accounting Vol 1 distillation (GAAP, revenue recognition five-step Topic 606, conservatism, full-disclosure principle) plus Business Ethics distillation (stakeholder pluralism, normative theories) plus Business Law distillation (fiduciary duty, fraud triangle). A naive method will get the accounting mechanics or the ethical framing but rarely both well; a corpus method has the chapters but not the integrated lens.

A long-term software customer has just signed a $1.2M three-year contract. Our SaaS product has a meaningful new-feature rollout scheduled for month 4. The customer's CFO has indicated, off the record, that they expect the new feature to be functional from day one — but our contract is silent on the new feature and our delivery team thinks it is unlikely to be ready until month 5 or 6.

Our CFO wants to recognise the full year-one revenue at signing. Our auditor will look at this in Q4. The customer relationship is strategic — we cannot afford to lose them, and if they perceive we have over-promised they may walk. Our sales team's commission cycle ends next month.

How should we think about the revenue recognition? How should we think about the customer disclosure? How should we think about the internal commission decision? Where is the line between sharp practice and reasonable judgement? Cite the standards and frameworks you are drawing on.
