# Strategic decision under environmental uncertainty

**Domain:** decision-making
**Strength claim:** The Principles-of-Management distillation consolidates SWOT, PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, VRIO, Mintzberg structures, and the strategic management cycle into one routable distillation. A naive method is likely to deliver a textbook SWOT/PESTEL summary without the integration; a research method may surface contemporary case examples but not the diagnostic frame.

We are a 200-person professional services firm considering whether to acquire a 40-person specialist firm in an adjacent practice area. The target is profitable, well-regarded, and would give us cross-sell opportunity into a sector we have struggled to enter organically. The deal is ~25% of our annual revenue at the asking price. Three concerns: (1) the founder-leader is part of the value and indicates she will leave 18 months post-acquisition; (2) the target's clients are highly relationship-driven and concentrated; (3) two of our existing senior partners have privately expressed reservations about the cultural fit.

Walk me through the strategic-management framework I should be using to evaluate this acquisition. What are the right diagnostic questions for each of the three concerns? What are the structural integration issues we should be planning for now, before we sign? What are the realistic failure modes and the early-warning signals for each? Cite the frameworks you are using.
